## Design Specificity Verdict

**Product-authored behavior, only partially authored visual language.**

The experience is distinctly Neo Anki where it matters most: time-budgeted learning, protected due work, subject blocks, recovery plans, prompt health, safe local restore, and the humane ending “That was enough for this session.” Those choices could not be transplanted unchanged into an unrelated product.

The visual language is less proprietary. Cream surfaces, restrained purple, Lucide icons, thin borders, compact toolbars, and standard card/table compositions could also serve a notes app or project manager. The strongest identity currently lives in language and workflow rather than typography, composition, or a recognizable study motif.

The deterministic app-scope scan found **6 warnings and 0 errors** in `src/styles.css`: four `layout-transition`, one `overused-font`, and one `side-tab`. Five are stale or contextual false positives: three animation selectors are unused, the early Inter declaration is overridden by the later native system stack, and the side accent is a help paragraph rather than a tab/card. The only live technical finding is the Review progress bar’s width transition at `src/styles.css:202`; it is valid but low risk.

No reliable user-visible detector overlay was produced because Codex Browser’s page evaluation surface is read-only. Browser screenshot, DOM, computed-style, console, and CLI JSON evidence were used instead.

## Overall Impression

Neo Anki is calmer, safer, and more humane than most spaced-repetition software. It explains workload in time and subjects rather than scheduler machinery, and it consistently reassures users that unfinished work and imported data are safe. The biggest opportunity is to make the administrative surfaces as focused as the study flow: capture, Library, and Settings currently reveal expert machinery before it is needed.

## What’s Working

1. **Product-specific reassurance.** Today and Review turn spaced repetition into understandable time, subjects, and recovery instead of opaque scheduling statistics.
2. **Safety is part of the interface.** Preserved drafts, recoverable Trash, Undo, retries, checkpoints, and precise failure copy make “local-first” credible.
3. **Novice and expert paths coexist.** Plain-language actions sit alongside keyboard shortcuts, custom study, saved views, bulk operations, and extension surfaces.

## Cognitive Load

**Moderate: 3 of 8 checklist failures.**

- **Chunking fails in Library:** browse mode, search, collection, sort, health filter, saved views, and Add knowledge share one control band.
- **Minimal choices fail during bulk selection:** more than ten actions appear together.
- **Progressive disclosure fails in Create and Settings:** capture exposes content model, templates, metadata, citations, preview, and quality checks; Settings stacks unrelated systems in one long modal.

The most overloaded decision points are Empty Today (daily target plus four competing paths), Library’s default toolbar, Library bulk actions, and Settings. Onboarding workload and Review grading both cap choices at four and should be preserved as models.

## Emotional Journey

The product’s refusal to shame the learner is excellent. “How much time can learning reliably have?”, “recovery plans—not failures,” “Anything unfinished stays in the queue,” and “That was enough for this session” create reassurance exactly where study products often create guilt.

The emotional valleys are operational: Empty Library looks administrative before it contains knowledge; New Knowledge feels like configuring a data model; Settings exposes too much machinery; and the completion visual treatment does not fully match the strength of its copy. Restore and import moments are especially strong because validation, checkpoints, explicit confirmation, and “current data has not changed” make high-stakes actions feel safe.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Basic capture is buried inside knowledge-model configuration

**Why it matters:** A first-time user sees content type, fields, template summary, collection, tags, citations, preview, and prompt health before saving. “Remember this” becomes “configure a card system.”

**Fix:** Make Prompt and Answer the immediate path. Collapse metadata, citations, templates, extension actions, and advanced quality information under progressive disclosure.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable distill`

### [P1] Library becomes an operations console before it becomes a library

**Why it matters:** Empty Library retains filters and sort controls, while selection reveals more than ten bulk actions. Users must scan administration before rediscovering knowledge.

**Fix:** Keep browse mode and search primary. Move secondary filters into a popover with applied-filter chips. Group bulk actions into Study, Organize, Schedule, and More. Hide irrelevant controls in empty states.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable layout`

### [P2] The visual identity is quieter than the product idea

**Why it matters:** The polished cream/purple system is category-interchangeable; without copy, little visually says memory, retrieval, rhythm, or time-aware learning.

**Fix:** Introduce one restrained, reusable Neo Anki motif around memory strength, time budget, or retrieval rhythm, focused on Today and session completion rather than decorating every surface.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable delight`

### [P2] The mental model has too many neighboring nouns

**Why it matters:** “Knowledge item,” “practice prompt,” “card template,” “native card,” “review,” “recall target,” and “content type” are individually defensible but collectively expensive for newcomers.

**Fix:** Establish one canonical noun for what users author and one for what they study. Reserve scheduler and template terms for advanced settings.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable clarify`

### [P2] Settings lacks information architecture

**Why it matters:** Appearance, templates, sync, retention, safeguards, storage, import/export, diagnostics, recovery, and destructive actions share one long panel. Users cannot form a stable map, and danger/data actions lose spatial separation.

**Fix:** Add categories or make Settings a dedicated page. Separate learning, cards, sync/data, extensions, appearance, and danger controls.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable layout`

## Persona Red Flags

**Alex — Power User**

- Strong shortcuts, Library search, review keys, Undo, custom study, and bulk actions.
- One-item-at-a-time authoring limits throughput.
- The bulk toolbar is so crowded that scanning erodes the efficiency it is meant to provide.
- No command palette consolidates expert actions.

**Jordan — First-Timer**

- Onboarding’s two initial paths and four workload choices are clear and reassuring.
- New Knowledge immediately introduces content type, templates, and prompt health.
- Empty Library shows administrative controls before content exists.
- No visible Help or glossary explains item versus prompt versus card.

**Sam — Accessibility-Dependent User**

- Strong skip link, focus rings, semantic labels, modal roles, route focus management, live regions, 44px controls, and keyboard review flow.
- Compact Library selects rely on current values while labels are visually hidden.
- `.62–.72rem` metadata and faint text are demanding for low-vision users.
- Dense tables and bulk controls create long linear keyboard paths.

## Minor Observations

- Desktop toolbar titles duplicate page H1s without adding much orientation.
- Empty Library should hide sort and health filters until content exists.
- The all-sans desktop typography is crisp but does little to express the reflective, memory-centered tone.
- The onboarding safety footnote is useful but too small and partly repetitive.
- Several secondary gray actions become visually indistinct when grouped.
- “Local-first memory system” is strong positioning but disappears after onboarding.
- Review completion copy is excellent; its visual peak could reward progress more strongly without gamification.
- Replace the live Review progress width transition with a transform-based fill if animation performance becomes measurable.
